What it is

Fixed geometry, predictable results

iCort Pro is designed to anchor capture in a known physical configuration, so sessions behave consistently across time, devices, and operators.

We describe the capabilities, not the enabling mechanics.

Calibration as a precondition

The pipeline is designed to depend on verified physical calibration. When calibration compliance is absent, the system can restrict or degrade operation by design.

Deterministic behavior is the goal: fewer “it depends” moments.

Core pillars

P1

Monolithic fixed-geometry hardware

A rigid capture platform designed to maintain a repeatable spatial relationship between capture nodes.

P2

Adapter-based sensor normalization

Device-specific interfaces intended to normalize heterogeneous capture devices into a common physical frame.

P3

Physical enforcement of optical state

Mechanisms intended to enforce or verify a known optical configuration prior to capture.

P4

Calibration-dependent pipeline

An end-to-end capture and processing pipeline intended to consume hardware-derived calibration without performing software estimation.
