What it is

A hardware-anchored approach to spatial capture that treats geometry stability as the foundation. The system is designed to make results more repeatable and easier to validate step by step.

Fixed Geometry

The relationship between sensors is enforced physically, aiming for stable extrinsics and consistent capture conditions.

Calibration First

Calibration is treated as a controlled prerequisite, not something re-estimated continuously during capture.

Validation Driven

The goal is measurable consistency: repeatable results, predictable behavior, and clear verification steps.

Why this approach

Many spatial pipelines rely on probabilistic estimation that can drift or vary across runs. A hardware-enforced baseline aims to reduce variance and improve confidence in the resulting spatial media.

Problem signals

  • Drift and low repeatability between sessions
  • Sensitivity to scene texture and operator behavior
  • Calibration complexity and human error risk
  • Hard-to-verify outcomes in real-world capture

Intended outcomes

  • More repeatable capture conditions
  • Improved metrical consistency where applicable
  • Clear, testable workflow steps
  • A practical base for further pipeline development
